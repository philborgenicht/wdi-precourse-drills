What's It All About?

When I first applied for Galvanize, I kind of kept it a secret from many of my close friends and family members.
By no means was I embarrassed, quite the opposite actually. I was proud of myself, as were my mother, sister, and brother in law,
only one of whom actually has a coding background.For years I had kind of been stuck in a job that I was good at and did enjoy,
but from which I really derived no sense of personal fulfillment, nor did I really see much if any room for advancement or upward mobility.
When I went to college about 8 years ago, I did take some introductory computer science and some html/javascript, and I did enjoy them,
but I never really pursued it as a career path, for reasons of which I am unaware. I graduated with a bachelor's degree in accounting,
but I never really used it and just wound up working at a Best Buy store, got complacent, and never tried for something more.

Earlier this year, I did apply for a management position with that company, but when I did not receive it, I finally decided to apply
for Galvanize to make something more of myself and pursue a better career.I was lucky enough to have the support of my immediate family
to convince me that this was the right thing to do, becuase changing jobs or careers at any time in life can be somewhat stressful,
but I had them to reassure me that I was making the right choice. When I submitted the application, received the portfolio instructions,
read through "Javascript For Kids", and completed lessons on Code Academy, I was sure I had made the right choice.By no means do I think the
next six months will be easy, but I know the knowledge I will gain, and career paths that I will open up will be well worth the hard work and sacrifice.

Where Do I Go From Here

Obviously, the main reason anyone starts a career or switches to a new career is partially for financial reasons. I know that even an entry level coder or web deeloper can make a decent salary and with years of hard work and improvement, building a strong skill set, and being able to show a proven track record of effective work, one can do fairly well. At 29 years old, I still like to think of myself as relatively "young" per se, and I want to do my best to learn, work hard, and improve for the rest of my life. Some concrete goals I have come up with include the following

  1. Graduate Galvanize in the top 10% percent of my cohort
  2. Secure a Job with a small to midsize firm upon of graduation
  3. Secure a job with a large, well known company like Facebook or Google within 3-5 years
  4. Start a family, for whom I can provide a good life
  5. Travel to different countries around the world, to experience different people, food, and cultures
  6. Last but not least, be happy with my career, with myself, and make my family and friends proud
